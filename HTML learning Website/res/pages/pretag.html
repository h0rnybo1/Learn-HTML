Introduction to the <pre> Tag

The <pre> (preformatted text) tag is used to display text exactly as it is written in the HTML code. This means it retains all spaces, line breaks, and tabs. This tag is especially useful for displaying things like code, poetry, or other text where the formatting is important.

Common Uses of the <pre> Tag

  • Displaying code snippets
  • Writing poems
  • Formatting letters
  • Showing ASCII art

Without the <pre> tag, maintaining the exact spacing and formatting of the text would be challenging. Here is an example of a poem formatted using the <pre> tag:

                O my Love is like a red, red rose
                Thats newly sprung in June;
                O my Love is like the melody
                Thats sweetly played in tune.

                    So fair art thou, my bonnie lass,
                    So deep in luve am I;
                    And I will luve thee still, my dear,
                    Till a the seas gang dry.

                Till a the seas gang dry, my dear,
                And the rocks melt wi the sun;
                I will love thee still, my dear,
                While the sands o life shall run.

                    And fare thee weel, my only luve!
                    And fare thee weel awhile!
                    And I will come again, my luve,
                    Though it were ten thousand mile.
